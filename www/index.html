Manifold Clustering

The problem is multi-manifold clustering, which aims to label each node according to the manifold it came from. The current methodologies used in the literature to distinguish the points from the different intersecting manifolds are too complicated to show consistency. Our method comes from the smoothness intuition to determine if local points are on the same manifold.

For example, if the intersecting manifolds are two intersecting circles like the following, it is hard to find a simple algorithm with consistenct properties to distinguish the points. On the contrary, our method can distinguish them and show it is simple enough to show the consistency.

About

Our algorithm can show the convergence rate and as well as the global consistency. Our method comes from the smoothness intuiion to determine if two points are on the same manifold. If the geodesic between them is smooth enough, then we can think they are on the same manifold. We need to regularize the path to prevent the zigzag. In all, our methodology is simple enough to show the consistency.

Under the supervision of Professor Nicolas Garcia Trillos, I collaborat with a classmate Pengfei He to work on this manifold clustering algorithm.
